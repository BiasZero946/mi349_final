The Original Pitchboard

In the original pitchboard of Wapipely, it was intended to be a site where people who were looking for a job could submit their portfolios onto the site, after which the employers who were looking to hire people could filter through the skills and kinds of positions they need to be filled until they were able to easily find and reach out to new potential employees. It was going to be a competitor to sites such as LinkedIn. However, there were some issues with the original concept, such as not letting the users upload their resumes and not being very well formatted.

The New Pitchboard

In the New Pitchboard several things were changed from the original. The biggest change is that users are now also able to upload their resume as well as their portfolio in order to allow the different employers to see what skills they have as well as what companies they have worked for previously as well. Another change from the original pitchboard design is that there is some better design, such as the different colored backgrounds on the most important parts in order to draw the viewers eyes better. The last change was that Wapipely went from a site with multiple users to a site with a single user due to the lack of a server others could upload to.

Portfolio Wireframe

The first page of the site would be of the user's portfolio pieces that they have uploaded to the site. At the top of the page the site will have the title of the current page the user is on, as well as a link to the other page on the site for ease of navigation for the user. Each picture on the site will have a caption under it giving a brief description of what it is. Beneath the pictures in between them will be a brief description of the game, what parts I created, and a link to the page of the game for the users to see. Finally, at the very bottom will be a Contact Me area, where the users will be able to see my email addresses or any other information that they can use to contact me directly.

Resume Wireframe

The second page of the site will be for the users resume and skills. At the top of the page there is a link back to the Portfolio page, as well as the title of the page the user is currently on. Below them is a download link to a PDF of the resume the client has so that if the user wants a quick summary of their previous experience, it's readily available. After that the user will be able to see the Education of the client as well as some of the skills they have mastered. The user will then be able to see a more in-depth description of what the client did at each of their previous companies. Lastly, there is the Contact Me area at the bottom of the page.
